Basal body temperature (BBT) tracking is a natural, cost-effective method to help you understand your menstrual cycle and identify when ovulation occurs. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know.

## What is Basal Body Temperature?

Your basal body temperature is your lowest body temperature during rest. It's typically measured first thing in the morning before you get out of bed or engage in any physical activity.

## How BBT Changes During Your Cycle

Throughout your menstrual cycle, your BBT follows a predictable pattern:

1. **Follicular Phase (Pre-ovulation)** - BBT remains relatively low, typically between 97.0°F and 97.5°F (36.1°C to 36.4°C)

2. **Ovulation** - Right after ovulation, progesterone causes a noticeable temperature rise

3. **Luteal Phase (Post-ovulation)** - BBT rises by 0.5°F to 1°F (0.3°C to 0.6°C) and stays elevated until your next period

## How to Track Your BBT

### Step 1: Get the Right Thermometer

Use a basal body thermometer, which measures temperature to two decimal places (e.g., 97.52°F). Regular thermometers aren't precise enough for BBT tracking.

### Step 2: Take Your Temperature Consistently

- Take your temperature at the same time every morning
- Measure before getting out of bed or moving around
- Try to get at least 3 hours of sleep before measuring

### Step 3: Record Your Data

Use a chart or app to log your daily temperature. Over time, you'll see a pattern emerge.

## Tips for Accurate BBT Tracking

> Remember: BBT tracking confirms ovulation after it happens. It's best used alongside other methods like cervical mucus monitoring for predicting fertile days.

- Take your temperature before talking or drinking
- Note any factors that might affect your temperature (illness, alcohol, poor sleep)
- Track for at least 3 cycles to identify your pattern

## When BBT Tracking Works Best

BBT tracking is most effective for:

- Understanding your cycle patterns over time
- Confirming that ovulation has occurred
- Identifying your luteal phase length
- Planning pregnancy naturally
